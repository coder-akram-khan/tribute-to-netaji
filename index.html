Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose

23 January 1897 - 18 August 1945

"Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom."

The Immortal Patriot

The Awakening

Born into a prosperous Bengali family in Cuttack, Odisha, young Subhash showed exceptional brilliance from childhood. His encounter with nationalist ideas during his studies ignited a fire that would never be extinguished. Rejecting a comfortable civil services career, he chose the thorny path of India's liberation.

The Revolutionary

Where others saw impossibility, Netaji saw opportunity. His fearless leadership, strategic brilliance, and unwavering determination transformed him from a young rebel into one of India's most formidable freedom fighters. He believed that freedom was not to be begged for‚Äîit had to be seized.

The Visionary

Netaji's vision extended beyond mere political independence. He dreamed of a strong, self-reliant India where every citizen enjoyed equality and justice. His progressive ideas on women's empowerment, social equality, and economic self-sufficiency were decades ahead of his time.

Journey of Courage

1897

Born on 23rd January in Cuttack, Orissa, to Janakinath Bose and Prabhavati Devi

1920

Resigned from Indian Civil Services to join the freedom movement, inspired by Mahatma Gandhi's call

1938-39

Elected President of Indian National Congress, advocating complete independence

1941

Escaped house arrest and made a daring journey to Germany via Afghanistan

1943

Reorganized the Indian National Army (Azad Hind Fauj) in Singapore

1943

Established the Provisional Government of Free India (Azad Hind Sarkar)

1944

Led the INA in the historic march to Delhi with the rallying cry "Chalo Dilli!"

Eternal Legacy

Netaji's sacrifice ignited a revolution that shook the British Empire to its core. His fearless leadership, uncompromising patriotism, and revolutionary vision continue to inspire millions. The Indian National Army trials sparked nationwide protests, forcing the British to realize that India could no longer be held in bondage. Though he may have departed physically, his spirit lives eternally in every Indian heart that beats for freedom, dignity, and national pride.
